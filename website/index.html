Dynamic gestures from the wrist

EgoWrist-Gesture30 captures complementary visual and inertial evidence for mobile, natural hand interaction.

Abstract

Wrist-worn cameras enable always-available gesture input, but observe the hand from a moving and partially occluded viewpoint. EgoWrist-Gesture30 provides two complementary RGB streams oriented toward the dorsal and palm sides of the hand, together with WT61C-TTL inertial measurements. The dataset contains 30 dynamic gesture classes performed by 13 participants across 336 indoor and outdoor recordings. Canonical frame-level temporal annotations and balanced derived segments support gesture recognition, cross-user evaluation, reliability-aware multi-view learning, and multimodal sensor-fusion research.

Dorsal RGB

A wrist-mounted camera observes the back of the hand and captures complementary motion and articulation cues.

Palm RGB

A second wrist-mounted camera observes the palm side, reducing self-occlusion through a complementary viewpoint.

Inertial sensing

WT61C-TTL measurements provide acceleration, angular velocity, and Euler-angle signals for multimodal research.

Thirty dynamic interaction primitives

The vocabulary spans finger articulation, fist and palm transitions, directional motion, symbolic actions, and whole-hand gestures.

Data distribution

The balanced representation contains 4,255 temporal segments. Original intervals remain the canonical annotation source.

Dual-view wrist-worn device

Two 160-degree fixed-focus cameras capture the dorsal and palm views at a nominal 12 FPS. A shared polling loop provides software-based approximate synchronization.

Research-ready annotations and tools

The GitHub repository hosts temporal annotations, acquisition software, processing utilities, quality-review tools, and integrity validators. Recorded RGB and IMU data are distributed separately.

Inclusive temporal intervals

Each recording CSV stores begin, end, and zero-based label fields. Both frame boundaries are inclusive.

No prescribed split

The release does not impose training, validation, or test partitions. Participant-disjoint protocols are recommended for cross-user evaluation.

Access the dataset

Annotations and research tools are versioned on GitHub. Recorded data access metadata and integrity information are maintained with the release.
